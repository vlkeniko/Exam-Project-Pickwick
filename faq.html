Frequently Asked Questions



You can find the current Pickwick range on our website. Pickwick is constantly innovating and looking closely at what consumers want. We know that people have different tea needs and we also respond to new trends with our products. Limited shelf space in supermarkets also means that we have to make choices with regard to the products we want to offer consumers. This sometimes results in range changes, whereby we remediate products on the one hand, but also regularly add new variants on the other. For this reason, certain tea varieties are removed from our range. We will no longer have stock of these variants.
No sugars are added to the varieties from the Pickwick tea range, with the exception of Pickwick Peach and Rooibos Mango Peach. Soon these variants will no longer contain added sugars, because the current peach piece will be replaced by a piece without sugar. All variants with fruit and aromas contain on average less than 0.1g sugar per 100 ml. Variants such as Pickwick English Tea Blend, Ceylon, Green Tea Pure, Rooibos original and pure herbal variants such as chamomile do not contain sugar

Tea contains caffeine just like coffee. A cup of tea contains about half as much caffeine as a cup of coffee, but still so much that it has an invigorating effect on the brain. The advantage of the lower caffeine concentration in tea is that you do not get too much caffeine so easily. Too much caffeine can for some result in e.g. internal turmoil and other side effects.
Due to the caffeine content, tea can be important for pregnant women. High caffeine intake is suspected to have some detrimental effects associated with pregnancy. Hinds et al. (1996) conclude in a review article that caffeine intake of at least 300 mg per today is associated with a slightly lower birth weight, which may be particularly relevant for premature babies and children with already low birth weights.
Both black tea and green tea are based on leaves from the tea bush Camellia Sinensis, but black tea is tea leaves that are dried and fermented (fermented), while green tea is tea leaves that are simply dried. Green tea is not oxygenated as much as black tea and therefore a large part of the natural vitamins, minerals and antioxidants are preserved.
